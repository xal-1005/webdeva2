MMA Crash Course

Brief History

MMA is a full contact combat sport that combines rulesets from many different martial arts. Originally, gimmick fights pitting fighters of different styles against each other (such as Rick Roufus representing Kickboxing against Changpuek Kietsongrit representing Muay Thai) were held and promoted. Many of such fights were held in the 1900s, until an organisation called the Ultimate Fighting Championship was formed and popularised this sport by introducing it to the West. The first ever UFC event was a barbaric tournament style competition that consisted of no rules, a low quality cage, no protective gear and no weight classes. This resulted in some hilarious matchups. After several decades, MMA has evolved to become the sport that we know it as today.

Where is it practiced?

MMA is practiced internationally. There are many MMA promotions ranging from major ones such as UFC and ONE Championship, to lesser known local competitions.

Kickboxing

A striking style that combines boxing with various kicking techniques. This is an extremely effective style that has taken fighters to championship titles, as it is well-rounded and teaches many ways to defend against most striking styles. Kickboxers are feared for their speed and power. Notable fighters that use Kickboxing include Israel Adesanya and Alex Pereira.

Muay Thai

The Art of Eight Limbs is one of the most dangerous martial arts in the world, as it nearly maximises every single way the human body can be used as a striking weapon and pushes a fighter's conditioning and pain tolerance to the highest of levels. Similar to kickboxing but with a more fluid stance, Muay Thai adds elbows, knees, and takedowns to their arsenal. Elbows easily slice open skin while knees act as sledgehammers that pound through your opponents. Notable Nak Muay include Suphachai "Saenchai" Saepong, Buakaw Banchamek, and Rodtang Jitmuangnon.

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

A style that might seem odd to bystanders at first, BJJ is a style that demands respect from anyone who has grappled with an experienced BJJ practitioner. Focusing on takedowns, grappling, submissions, chokeholds, etc., BJJ is a style that allows a lighter opponent to still deal damage to a much heavier opponent. Its practitioners are like human pythons that coil themselves around their opponents and find a windpipe or any joint that can be exploited to inflict excruciating pain. Notable BJJ practitioners include the Gracie Family and Mikey Musumeci.

Fedor Emelianenko

Even though he has never fought in the UFC, Emelianenko is widely regarded as one of the best MMA fighters of all time. He had a 27 fight win-streak that spanned over 10 years, and was the PRIDE Heavyweight Champion from 2003 to 2007.

Jon Jones

An infamous fighter that has been caught using steroids, using cocaine, and abusing his wife, his MMA record is as illustrious as his criminal record. Fighting in the UFC since 2008, his only loss ever was by unintentionally throwing an illegal elbow at his opponent. Regarded as the most dangerous man on planet Earth right now, he is the former UFC Light Heavyweight World Champion and the current Heavyweight World Champion.

Israel Adesanya

Dominating the UFC Middleweight division from 2019-2022, Israel Adesanya defeated every ranked contender in his weight class, which included former champions. However, his recent actions have caused much controversy.

Alex Pereira

Fueled by Adesanya's disrespectful comments towards him, Alex Pereira moved from GLORY Kickboxing to the UFC, just to prove to Adesanya that he was still a formidable foe. In just two years, Pereira knocked out ranked contenders and took the Middleweight Title from Adesanya, and then moved up a weight class and won the Light Heavyweight Title. He is one of only three fighters to be inducted into the GLORY Hall of Fame.

Conor Mcgregor

Taking the featherweight division by storm and knocking out the defending champion in thirteen seconds, Conor Mcgregor is undoubtedly the most influential MMA fighter ever, helping to revolutionise this sport and make it an international sensation. He is the first ever fighter to hold Championship titles in two different weight classes simultaneously. His lightweight title fight against Khabib Nurmagomedov is the most bought Pay-Per-View MMA event of all time.
